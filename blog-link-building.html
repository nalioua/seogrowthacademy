7 LINK BUILDING TECHNIQUES THAT STILL WORK IN 2025

Backlinks remain one of Google's most powerful ranking signals. But the strategies that worked in 2018 can get your site penalized today. Here are 7 techniques that work right now — and exactly how to implement each one.

📅 March 8, 2025 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ SEO Growth Academy 📂 Link Building · Off-Page SEO

Ask any SEO expert what the single most important off-page ranking factor is and they will say the same thing: backlinks. A backlink is a link from another website pointing to yours. In Google's eyes, each backlink is a vote of confidence — a signal that your content is worth referencing.

But here's what most guides won't tell you: not all backlinks are equal. A link from a trusted, relevant website in your industry is worth exponentially more than hundreds of links from random, low-quality directories. In 2025, link building is about earning authority — not gaming the system.

"One high-quality backlink from a relevant, authoritative website in your niche will do more for your rankings than 200 low-quality links combined. Always prioritize quality over quantity."

WHY BACKLINKS STILL MATTER IN 2025

Despite the rise of AI search and Google's many algorithm updates, backlinks remain a core ranking signal. Here is why they still matter:

  • They signal trust — other websites are essentially vouching for your content
  • They drive referral traffic directly — not just SEO benefits
  • They help Google discover and index your new pages faster
  • They are a strong signal for GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity use backlinks and brand mentions to decide which sites to recommend

THE 7 TECHNIQUES THAT WORK IN 2025

1. Strategic Guest Posting

Find 5-10 blogs in your industry that publish content for your target audience and accept guest contributions. Pitch a genuinely useful article — not a promotional piece. Include one natural, contextual link to your most valuable page. Quality and relevance are everything here.

How to find opportunities: Search Google for "[your niche] write for us" or "[your niche] guest post". Use ChatGPT to write a personalized outreach email for each blog.

2. The Skyscraper Technique

Find a popular, well-linked article in your niche. Create a significantly better version — more comprehensive, more current, better structured. Then reach out to every website linking to the original and show them your superior version. Many will update their link to point to yours.

Free tool to find linked content: Ahrefs Backlink Checker (free version) shows you who links to any URL.

3. Unlinked Brand Mentions

People are probably already mentioning your business online without linking to you. Set up a free Google Alert for your brand name. Every time someone mentions you without a link, send a polite email asking them to add one. Since they already know you, response rates are high — often 20-30%.

💡 Free Outreach Email Template

Hi [Name], I noticed you mentioned [Your Brand] in your article "[Article Title]" — thank you! Would you be open to adding a link to our site so your readers can learn more? Here is our URL: [URL]. Happy to return the favor by sharing your article with our audience.

4. Create Free, Linkable Resources

The best backlink magnet is a genuinely useful free resource. Think calculators, templates, checklists, guides, and datasets. For a Shopify store, this could be a product pricing calculator, a free SEO checklist for e-commerce, or a guide to shipping costs by country. People naturally link to tools they find useful.

5. Digital PR and Original Data

Publish a study, survey, or original data about your industry. Even surveying 50 customers can generate interesting statistics worth publishing. Journalists and bloggers constantly search for data to cite — and when they cite you, they link to you.

Reach out to journalists in your niche via platforms like HARO (Help A Reporter Out) — a free service where reporters ask for expert sources and link to them in their articles.

6. Podcast Guest Appearances

Reach out to podcasts in your industry as a guest expert. Most podcast episodes include show notes with a link to the guest's website. These links are highly contextual, often from trusted domains, and you also get direct exposure to a new engaged audience.

Start with smaller podcasts in your niche — they are much easier to get on and just as valuable for link building when you are starting out.

7. Genuine Community Contributions

Participate actively in Reddit communities, Quora, LinkedIn groups, and niche forums. Provide genuinely helpful, detailed answers. When your own content is the most helpful resource for a question, link to it naturally. Never spam or self-promote without adding value — you will get banned and it will hurt your reputation.

WHAT DESTROYS YOUR SEO IN 2025

These tactics used to work but will now get your site penalized or de-indexed by Google:

  • Buying backlinks — Google's spam detection is sophisticated and improving every month
  • Private Blog Networks (PBNs) — networks of fake sites created solely to pass links
  • Reciprocal link schemes — mass "I'll link to you if you link to me" arrangements with unrelated sites
  • Spammy directory submissions — bulk submissions to low-quality link directories
  • Comment spam — leaving links in blog comments unrelated to your content

"Google penalizes manipulative link building — and recovering from a manual penalty can take 6-12 months. Focus on earning links through value, not tricks."

HOW TO TRACK YOUR BACKLINKS FOR FREE

Use these free tools to monitor your link profile:

  • Google Search Console → Links report shows all sites linking to you
  • Ahrefs Free Backlink Checker → Shows top backlinks for any domain
  • Ubersuggest → Free backlink analysis with domain authority scores

Check your backlink profile monthly. If you see a sudden spike of low-quality links pointing to your site (sometimes competitors do this to harm you), you can disavow them using Google's Disavow Tool.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How many backlinks do I need to rank on Google? +
There is no magic number. Quality matters far more than quantity. 10 backlinks from trusted, relevant websites in your niche will outperform 500 low-quality links every time. Focus on building relationships with authoritative sites in your industry.
Is link building still important for SEO in 2025? +
Yes. Backlinks remain one of Google's top 3 ranking factors in 2025. Additionally, backlinks and brand mentions are now important for GEO — appearing in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews.
Can I build backlinks for free? +
Absolutely. Guest posting, unlinked brand mentions, community contributions, podcast appearances, and creating shareable resources are all free strategies. They require time and effort but zero budget — perfect for small businesses just starting out.
